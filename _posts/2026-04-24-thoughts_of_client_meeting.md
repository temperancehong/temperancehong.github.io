---
title: "Remember: Your bosses are on your side when preping for client meeting"
date: 2026-04-24
---

So, it is now the eighth week I am in this startup. Things are going, not all smoothly, but generally
I am happy with it. I started my first client project solo on week 5, and here I am, at the end of week 8.
We had 2 client meetings with this client, and I shadowed the second one.

Preparations before the client meetings can be stressful: people like amazing results in numbers, examples in visuals and timeline that is met. Sometimes it is just desperating that you have built a new structure and it works evennot as well as their baseline. Well, in research it happens a lot, that's research, and in business, on top of all, there's a deadline that you need to present something that is valid, better than what it was before.

There are different focus point of you and your bosses. You care about technical details and implementations, they would need something to present to client that does a WOW effect. Of course, every party all care about the final thing: client success. 

So that's what I learned:


> In the eyes of a client

That is what you should always think of. They don't care about what structure you newly proposed, how you simplified their workflow (or not that much), but the final numbers: does this enhance their system in a way that they wanted, does this cost them a lot more just to build one small detail, and IS IT PRODUCTION READY.

**Your bosses might give you a lot of stress especially before deadlines, when stressed, just remember, it is not about you.** I see it as the transmission of anxiety: they want to keep their clients happy, more than you do. *Under the assumption that you have a great team,* they are on your side of providing the best experience with clients, not there to be on you about how bad you are doing. 

Keep calm, keep focused. The day will eventually pass.
